# Yearly Review — Life Audit

The biggest ritual. Runs once a year, typically late December or early January. Takes time — don't rush it.

**Read before starting:**
- `bujo/INDEX.md` (Annual Goals section)
- All monthly logs from the year
- `bujo/archive/` (completed projects)
- `bujo/SOMEDAY_MAYBE.md`
- `bujo/FUTURE_LOG.md`

---

## PART 1 — Annual Goals Scorecard

Read the Annual Goals from `INDEX.md`. For each goal, score it:

| Status | Symbol |
|--------|--------|
| Completed | ✓ |
| Strong progress | ↑ |
| Partial / inconsistent | → |
| Stalled / abandoned | ✗ |
| Carried forward | ○ |

Format in the year-end log:
```
ANNUAL GOALS [YEAR] — FINAL SCORECARD

✓ Complete and exhibit Keep Out series
↑ Launch Paradigm Shift exhibition (May)
✓ Complete Georgia Tech MSA
→ Build consistent fitness habit
○ [Goal carried from previous year]
```

Don't spin the results. If a goal stalled, say why, briefly.

---

## PART 2 — Themes of the Year

Ask: "Without looking at the goals — what defined this year for you? What were the big themes?"

Let the answer come naturally. Then ask:
- "What were you most proud of?"
- "What do you most regret?"
- "What surprised you?"
- "What did you learn about yourself?"

Record these as notes in the year-end log. These themes inform next year's goals.

---

## PART 3 — Projects Audit

Review `bujo/archive/` for the year — everything completed. Then review `bujo/projects/` for active projects.

For each:
- What actually got done? (Be specific — not "worked on photo book" but "completed editing 80 images, chose 40 for shortlist")
- What didn't? Why?
- Is it still alive heading into next year?

---

## PART 4 — What to Carry Forward / What to Leave Behind

Two lists. Be ruthless.

**Carry forward:**
- Projects with real momentum or genuine importance
- Habits that are working
- Relationships to invest in
- Skills to develop

**Leave behind:**
- Projects that keep reappearing without progress (zombie projects — kill them)
- Obligations taken on for the wrong reasons
- Goals that belong to who you were, not who you are now

Ask for each item on "leave behind": "What would it feel like to officially stop this? Does it feel like relief or regret?"
Relief → kill it.
Regret → carry it forward, but with a specific next action.

---

## PART 5 — Set Next Year's Goals

Goals must be:
- **Specific enough to know when they're done** (not "be healthier" — "complete the Keep Out series and submit to [gallery] by October")
- **3–7 maximum** — more than 7 and none of them are real
- **One sentence each**
- **Organised by life area if helpful**: creative, work, health, relationships, learning

Ask: "What would make next year a success? What would you need to have done, or become?"

Write them in `bujo/INDEX.md` at the top, replacing the old year's goals.

---

## PART 6 — Memento Mori (Annual)

End with the deepest version of the question:

> "If you had one year left, would you spend it the same way you're planning to spend this one?"

One honest answer. No pressure to be profound. Write it in the year-end log.

---

## Year-End Log

Create `bujo/reference/year-[YYYY].md` with:
- Annual Goals Scorecard
- Themes of the Year
- Projects Audit summary
- Carry Forward / Leave Behind lists
- New Annual Goals
- Memento Mori note

---

## Close

"Year closed. [X] goals completed. [Y] goals carrying forward. New goals for [year]: [list briefly]."

Done.
