# Mental Inventory

A CBT-informed reset tool. Three columns, gap analysis, ruthless cull. Use when you feel off-track, scattered, or when the monthly review demands it.

---

## The Three Columns

| Actually Doing | Should Be Doing | Want To Be Doing |
|----------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| What's actually consuming your time and attention right now. Honest — not aspirational. | What your commitments, goals, and system say you should be doing. | What you genuinely want to be doing, unconstrained by obligation. |

---

## How to Run It

Ask Anton to fill each column out loud or in writing. Stream of consciousness. Don't filter. Don't judge. Go column by column.

### Column 1 — Actually Doing

Prompts:
- "What have you actually done in the last two weeks? Not what you planned — what happened."
- "What's been eating your time and attention, even if you didn't mean it to?"
- "What have you been avoiding — and what have you been doing instead?"

### Column 2 — Should Be Doing

Prompts:
- "What do your annual goals say you should be working on?"
- "What would you feel guilty about if the month ended and you hadn't touched it?"
- "What's been migrating in your weekly log without getting done?"

### Column 3 — Want To Be Doing

Prompts:
- "If your obligations disappeared for a month, what would you actually do?"
- "What projects make you feel alive when you think about them?"
- "What keeps showing up in SOMEDAY_MAYBE that you can't quite kill?"

---

## Gap Analysis

After filling the columns, run the analysis. Name each gap plainly.

**Col 1 vs Col 2 — Execution gap**
Are you doing what you're supposed to be doing? If not — what's the actual resistance? Annoying? Unclear? No longer relevant?

**Col 2 vs Col 3 — Values gap**
Are your obligations aligned with what you actually care about? A big gap here is not a productivity problem — it's a life-design problem. Worth naming.

**Col 1 vs Col 3 — Fulfilment gap**
Is there any overlap between what you're doing and what you want to do? Zero overlap is a warning sign.

---

## The Cull

After the gap analysis, for each item in Col 2 that isn't in Col 1:

Ask: "Does this still belong in your life? Kill, keep with a specific plan, or move to SOMEDAY_MAYBE?"

Be ruthless. Carroll: "If it's not worth rewriting, it's not worth doing."

---

## Output

Write the completed table into `bujo/MONTHLY_LOG.md` (if running during monthly review) or into today's daily log (if running as a standalone reset).

Record the gap analysis as one-line observations below the table. No essays — one sentence per gap.

---

## Tone

Direct. State what the data shows. If the gap is obvious, name it: "You've migrated this project six weeks in a row and it never appears in Column 1. That's not a scheduling problem."
